Use a Gap-Cap to Embed Hardware In Your Next 3D Print

March 29, 2026

Embedding Hardware into 3D Prints: A Useful Technique

The process of embedding fasteners or other hardware into 3D prints is indeed a valuable technique, but it presents challenges, especially when dealing with large or non-flat objects. To overcome these hurdles, use a gap-cap.

A gap-cap is a specialized tool designed to facilitate the embedding of hardware into 3D prints, making the process easier and more reliable. This innovation simplifies the embedding process, ensuring that fasteners or hardware can be securely integrated into a variety of shapes and sizes, even those that are not flat.

By employing a gap-cap, users can achieve professional-looking results and avoid the complexities and risks associated with traditional embedding methods. Whether you're working on a model that includes intricate designs or a structure with unique shapes, a gap-cap offers a straightforward and effective solution.
