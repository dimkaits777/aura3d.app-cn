Recycling PLA and Other Plastic Waste with Compression Molding

Compression molding offers new ways to repurpose plastic waste, as seen in [future things]'s exploration of this method in a new video. After experimenting with low-tech approaches like a toaster oven and 3D printed molds, they delve deeper into how compression molding can be a more effective solution.

  • Compression molding, a method traditionally used for producing plastic components, can transform waste plastic into useful products with less energy consumption compared to extrusion or injection molding.
  • The video details the process of compressing plastic waste under high pressure to shape it, demonstrating how this technique can be adapted for various applications, from creating everyday objects to more complex, specialized components.
  • As part of their ongoing efforts to reduce waste, [future things] aims to educate and inspire others to consider this sustainable method of repurposing plastic waste using modern compression molding technology.
