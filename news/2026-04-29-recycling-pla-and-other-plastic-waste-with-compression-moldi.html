Recycling PLA and Other Plastic Waste with Compression Molding

April 29, 2026

Compression molding of PLA and other plastic waste is the focus of a new video from [future things]. The team follows up on their previous experimentation with low-tech methods, including a toaster oven and 3D printed molds, to showcase more advanced techniques for recycling plastic waste.

The video delves into the process of using compression molding, a technique that applies heat and pressure to plastic materials to form them into desired shapes. This method could potentially offer a more efficient and scalable solution for recycling various types of plastic waste, moving beyond the initial trials and demonstrating the practical applications of compression molding in waste management.

Future developments in this area could significantly contribute to reducing plastic waste and promoting sustainable practices, making it a promising area for further research and innovation. Read the original article here.
