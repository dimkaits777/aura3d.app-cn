3D Print Becomes Cast Iron Wrench Via Microwave

March 27, 2026

3D Print Becomes Cast Iron Wrench Via Microwave

Consumer-grade 3D printing is great for prototyping and crafting soft plastic items. But when it comes to making durable, tough metal objects, nothing beats the strength of cast iron. A recent development shows how microwave technology can be leveraged to turn a simple 3D print into a cast iron wrench, demonstrating the versatility of modern manufacturing techniques.

  • Researchers have developed a process where metal powders are heated in a microwave to achieve a high degree of metallurgical purity. This purity is essential for achieving the strength and durability characteristic of cast iron.
  • By using this microwave-processed metal powder in a 3D printer, it's possible to create intricate metal objects with the hardness and strength of cast iron, all without the traditional high-temperature and high-pressure methods typically required for casting.
