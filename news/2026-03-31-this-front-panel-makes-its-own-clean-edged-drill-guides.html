This Front Panel Makes Its Own Clean-Edged Drill Guides

Bluesyann recently showcased an innovative instrument panel at a maker event. The unique panel was created by curing UV resin directly onto plywood, leveraging a 3D printer to form the intricate designs, resulting in clean-edged drill guides that require minimal cleanup. This method not only reduces the time spent on manual design but also allows for more precise and consistent patterns.

Maker Community Focus highlights the project as a testament to the capabilities of 3D printing in creating complex, custom designs. The project’s focus on precision and minimal cleanup has inspired other makers to experiment with similar techniques, pushing the boundaries of what can be achieved with UV resin and 3D printing.
