Improving FDM Filament Drying With a Spot of Vacuum

Researchers and manufacturers have found that incorporating a vacuum into the drying process can significantly improve the quality of fused deposition modeling (FDM) filament, according to a recent article on Hackaday.

The article discusses how a small vacuum can help remove residual moisture from filament, reducing the risk of warping and other quality issues that can plague FDM prints. By sealing the filament in a vacuum chamber, the drying process becomes more effective, ensuring that the material is as dry and stable as possible before use.
