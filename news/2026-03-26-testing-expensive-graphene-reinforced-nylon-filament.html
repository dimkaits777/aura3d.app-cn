Testing Expensive Graphene-Reinforced Nylon Filament

March 26, 2026

Testing Expensive Graphene-Reinforced Nylon Filament

The market for advanced 3D printing materials is constantly evolving, with new and innovative variants emerging all the time. One such variant is the PA12-based Lyten 3D graphene filament, which has recently been subjected to rigorous testing.

  • Lyten 3D, a pioneering company in 3D printing materials, has unveiled a new graphene-reinforced nylon filament designed to offer superior strength, durability, and enhanced performance over traditional nylon.
  • While the base material remains nylon, Lyten 3D has added graphene to the formulation, which is known for its exceptional electrical conductivity, thermal conductivity, and mechanical strength. This enhancement is expected to lead to the creation of stronger, more resilient 3D printed objects.
  • The testing process involved creating a variety of objects using both the standard nylon filament and the graphene-reinforced variant, comparing their strength, durability, and overall performance. Initial results suggest that the graphene-reinforced filament outperforms the standard version, especially in applications requiring high strength and flexibility.
