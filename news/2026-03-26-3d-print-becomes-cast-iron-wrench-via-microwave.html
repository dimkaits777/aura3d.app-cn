3D Print Becomes Cast Iron Wrench Via Microwave

March 26, 2026

3D Print Becomes Cast Iron Wrench Via Microwave

The potential of consumer-grade 3D printers has been stretched to new limits, with a recent innovation showing how 3D printing can be combined with unconventional methods to create durable metal objects.

While 3D printing excels in producing soft materials like plastics, researchers have found a way to leverage microwave technology to forge metal objects, a process that results in a cast iron-like quality. This breakthrough could pave the way for creating metal parts and tools at home, even when traditional methods are not an option. As always, this development underscores the ongoing evolution of 3D printing technology and its potential applications.
